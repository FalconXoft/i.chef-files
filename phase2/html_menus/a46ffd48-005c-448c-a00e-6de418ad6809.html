Daily Menu

Our menu changes daily according to what is fresh in the Fulton Fish Market that morning. Juan Lopez, chef, and buyer, hand picks his daily catch at 3:00 am and inspects his direct shipments of shellfish. His trucker leaves the market around 6 am and is delivered by 7 am to us. Our butchers are weighing the fish and preparing to start their busy day of cutting and portioning.

Fried Calamari with Marinara Sauce

Our calamari comes from Rhode Island—the source for the best squid in the country—and we sell about 300 pounds a week. The mix we serve is about three-quarters rings and one-quarter tentacles, but we do have customers who ask for all tentacles.

Caesar Salad

You could serve this hearty salad as a starter, but I think it makes a great lunch or a great light dinner. I like the additional kick that comes with using 7 anchovies in the dressing. We serve roasted garlic croutons made from focaccia

New England Clam Chowder

The recipe for our clam chowder dates back at least 100 years. It’s rich, creamy, and delicious—a real customer favorite. On cold winter days, we can sell as many 550 bowls of it.

Squid Salad

Rhode Island Point Judith Squid (Calamari) is the finest Squid in the world. Best tasting and most tender you will ever find. We source directly from the farmers. This salad has a balance of purple basil vinaigrette to excite the palate.

Oyster Panroast

We make our panroasts behind the bar, in silvery swivel pots that are heated by New York City steam. The pots get very hot, which means that we can make an oyster pan roast in just 2 1/2 minutes. In his book Arthur Schwartz’s New York City Food, food maven Arthur Schwartz says that the “Pan Roast of oysters and clams are among the oldest dishes still served in New York, dating from the first decade of the twentieth century, when Heinz ketchup and chili sauce were new, cutting edge ingredients.”

Oyster Stew

We make our oyster and seafood stews behind the bar, in silvery swivel pots that are heated by New York City steam. The pots get very hot, which means that we  can make an oyster stew in just 2 1/2 minutes. We have been making these delectable dishes since 1913.

Smoked Norwegian Smoked Salmon

Hand sliced Norwegian Smoked Salmon, served with non-pareil capers and toast points. A favorite here.

Bloody Mary Oyster Shooter

Fresh shucked Copps Island Bluepoint Oyster over fully spiced Bloody Mary with Vodka

Fried Oyster Po’ Boy Sandwich

This sandwich is our tribute to New Orleans

Maryland Crab Cakes

One of our favorite dishes. We now serve these delectable cakes daily. Jumbo lump Blue crab meat creates the sweetest crab cake. Served with lightly spicy marinara sauce.

Fried Ipswich Clams

We use only the most tender whole belly soft shell clams from Ipswich.

Grand Central Oyster Platter

One of our most popular raw bar items. We change this daily and give our customers an easy choice to make. 8 oysters total. 2 each of 2 varieties East and West coast oysters, plus 2 Littleneck and Cherrystone clams.

Grilled Salmon

We only use sustainable Norwegian farmed Salmon, flown in directly to The Fulton Fish Market daily.

Shrimp Cocktail

We only use Black Tiger jumbo shrimp for our Shrimp Cocktails. They are never from the Gulf.  These delicious gems are crisp, snappy and packed with a wonderful ocean flavour.

SwordFish

We only source Swordfish from certifiable waters. Our swordfish is always the fattiest and freshest available from the oceans. Plain grilled, pan fried, cajun style are just a few methods available.

Lobster Roll

We serve our version of this world-class sandwich with unsalted sweet potato chips. It’s a great combination. Other restaurants may use top-loading hot dog buns, but ours are split through the side—and they’re Martin’s potato rolls

Oysters Rockefeller

This is a core recipe here at the Oyster Bar—one of our signature dishes. Scott Conant featured this dish on the Food Network’s The Best Thing I Ever Ate, calling it “old-school cooking at its best.”  As he says, every component needs to be perfect .

Caviar Sandwich

Our Black Bowfin Caviar comes from a minor sustainable sturgeon in Louisiana. We serve it on white toast with chopped hard boiled eggs and a side of sour cream. One of our most popular sandwiches.

Fried Copps Island Bluepoint Oysters

Our shucked oysters are sourced from Copps Island, Conn. or the Chesapeake Bay daily and shipped directly to us to bread and fry. These plump delites are a crowd favorite.

Grilled Tuna

Most of the tuna we sell is bigeye. It has good fat content, a nice blood-red color, and a nice medium flavor. Yellowfin tuna is a good substitute for bigeye. It’s lighter in color and has a milder flavor, but still a good fat content. When buying tuna steaks, look for a bright color; it should never be brownish. Remove all the bloodline before cooking—it’s bitter.

Halibut

Fresh Bay of Fundy (Nova Scotia) or Maine Halibut is one of the most succulent gems of the seas. Mild, low in fat and flaky, Halibut is one of our most popular fish dishes.

Key Lime Pie

Our Key Lime Pie is the most flavorful pie anywhere you can find. Creamy and bursts with key lime flavor inside a graham cracker crust. Finished with a layer of fresh whipped cream.

Manhattan Clam Chowder

Our Manhattan Clam Chowder is a classic tomato based, lightly spicy chowder with all the goodies, lots of clams, potatoes and vegetables.

Covid Safety Precautions

WELLNESS CHECKS

  • All employees are required to stay home when sick.
  • All staff members are required to have their temperature checked. A temperature log will be kept for twenty-eight days.
  • All guests are required to have their temperature checked at the front door.
  • All guests are required to sanitize their hands upon entering the restaurant.
  • One guest member will be required to provide the restaurant with contact information for tracking if needed.

USE OF PPE

  • We will provide all staff members with masks, train them in the proper use and care of them to ensure compliance.
  • All guests will be are required to wear masks when not seated.

SOCIAL DISTANCING & ENHANCED SANITATION REQUIREMENTS

  • We have reduced our seating capacity to 25% to ensure physical distance is maintained across all areas of the restaurant.
  • 6 feet apart floor signs will be posted throughout the restaurant.
  • Different doors will be used for entering and leaving the restaurant for our staff and guests.
  • All restrooms’ occupancies will be monitored and sanitized every ½ hour.
  • Common areas will be deeply cleaned daily.
  • Tables & chairs will be cleaned and sanitized after each use.
  • Sanitizing stations will be placed through the restaurant.

TABLE SETTING

  • Tables will be set 6 feet apart to ensure social distancing.
  • One Time menu or contactless menus will be provided to every guest.
  • Each guest will be provided with sealed or wrapped utensils.
  • Contactless payment will be available.

COMMUNICATION & TRAINING

In a rapidly changing environment with heightened concern for both our staff and guests, we are committed to consistently communicate and educate them.
