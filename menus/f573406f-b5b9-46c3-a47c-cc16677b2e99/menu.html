A Refuge in the City

Named after Lido di Venezia, the famous beach in Italy, the playground of film stars, fashion icons, The Venice Film Festival, and of course, Josephine Baker in her heyday.

The food is memorably delicious, and the atmosphere is elegant but convivial. Our guests say they feel as if they’re at the home of a charming Italian friend.

Reviews

Review by - Yelp

five star review by Pallabi M:

Blown away. The food exceeded my expectations. You can't go wrong with the fried calamari and brussel sprouts which come with bacon and ricotta. Absolutely delicious. The squid ink pasta and spaghetti are two solid pasta options.

five star review by Ardmead D:

I ordered the Filet Mignon and it was excellent. The mushroom cream sauce was to die for. The staff was friendly. The bartender was very attentive even though he had a lot to do. I'm definitely going back to try more food.

five star review by Cat B:

Definitely my favorite Italian restaurant anywhere, ever! I've been here for several lunches and dinners and the drinks are creative and tasty, the service impeccable, and the food, oh the food is amazing with some healthy options

five star review by Kailynn V:

Lido is one of my favorites when it comes to good Harlem eats. It's a small intimate dining experience. In the sense of -- they'll sit you at a cute moderately sized table for two or three on the outside / inside their tables are decently sized for a size of 4-5 people. Cute little candles in the middle of the tables with a small bouquet of locally shopped flowers.
