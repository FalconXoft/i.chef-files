Grotta Azzurra, founded in 1908, is a legendary Italian restaurant in NYC's Little Italy. Named after the famous blue grotto in Capri, it became a favorite of stars like Sinatra and De Niro. After a hiatus, Grotta Azzurra has been revived by chef Frank Castellana, offering authentic Italian cuisine that transports guests to the Mediterranean.

A Piece of Italy in the Heart of New York
Authentic Italian Legacy
Founded by the Davino family, Grotta Azzurra was designed to capture the essence of the famous Blue Grotto on the island of Capri. The restaurant has been a cornerstone of Little Italy for over a century.
Legendary Patrons
Grotta Azzurra has hosted countless celebrities and luminaries over the decades, including Frank Sinatra, Robert De Niro, and Enrico Caruso, who would often dine here after performances.

The History of Grotta Azzurra
1
1908
Grotta Azzurra is founded by the Davino family, who wanted to bring the beauty and romance of Italy's Blue Grotto to New York City.
2
1930s
The restaurant becomes a hub for Italian-American culture and a favorite haunt of legendary celebrities like Frank Sinatra.
3
Present Day
Grotta Azzurra continues to preserve its rich history and tradition, offering guests an immersive Italian dining experience in the heart of Little Italy.
