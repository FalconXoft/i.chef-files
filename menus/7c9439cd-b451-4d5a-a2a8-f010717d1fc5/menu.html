Cipriani Wall Street 

Located in Lower Manhattan, Cipriani Wall Street stands as an icon of Greek Revival architecture featuring a magnificent Wedgwood dome and soaring ceilings. Now a grand and luxurious venue for events, this historical New York City landmark previously housed the New York Merchants’ Exchange, the New York Stock Exchange, the United States Customs House, and served as the headquarters of the National City Bank.

 

In the Financial District, steps from the New York Stock Exchange, and on one of the most notable streets in New York City, Cipriani Wall Street’s historic architecture and location makes it a unique and impressive setting for luxury events, charity galas, banquets, and exhibitions.

 

Cipriani Wall Street features a 12,275-square-foot ballroom that can accommodate up to 1,200 seated guests and boasts a spectacular mezzanine, perfect for pre-function gatherings. Cipriani Wall Street also offers adjacent breakout spaces such as a library for VIP meetings and dining, two terraces overlooking Wall Street, and many other green rooms and supporting breakout rooms. These spaces are well-suited for intimate gatherings or smaller meetings, ensuring versatility that accommodates a range of event types and capacities. Event planners can expect state-of-the-art amenities paired with exceptional service, making it an outstanding choice for those seeking to host upscale and memorable functions.
