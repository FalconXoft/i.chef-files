Is Halal Food Healthy? See Why More People Choose Al-Basha

  • by seoadmin
  • November 19, 2025
  • No Comments

Yes! Halal food is generally healthier because it follows strict dietary laws. It ensures cleanliness, ethical sourcing, and quality, making it the best for a nutritious and healthy diet. 

Halal food has numerous health benefits; it protects physical, mental, and spiritual health. The halal food completely avoids harmful ingredients that can be dangerous for the body, such as pork and alcohol. The core of halal food is that people eat food that benefits their bodies and improves their health holistically. 

In today’s guide, we’ll discuss halal food in detail, its taste, health, and nutritional benefits backed by science, and, last but not least, the concern many people have about whether halal food is humane. 

By the end of this blog, you’ll know how important it is to choose halal food and how beneficial it is for your health. 

What’s the Actual Definition of Halal Food?

Halal is an Arabic word, meaning allowed or permissible. It follows Islamic dietary rules according to religious texts and includes a great range of topics, from dealing with compassion with animals to the halal ingredients in your food, a thorough guide for every ingredient. 

Here are simple rules for Halal food according to Islamic guidelines: 

  1. The butcher must be Muslim or Ahl-e-Kitab (Jew or Christian).
  2. Bismillah Allahu Akbar must be said before slaughtering.
  3. The animal must be alive at the time of slaughter.
  4. A sharp knife should be used to minimize pain.
  5. The throat should be cut, the windpipe, the foodpipe, and the veins.
  6.  Blood must be allowed to flow out completely.
  7. The animal must be from a halal species (no pig or predator).
  8. Facing the Qiblah while slaughtering is Sunnah (recommended).
  9. The butcher must slaughter with mercy, not cruelty.
  10. No part of the animal should be eaten before proper slaughter.

Benefits of Halal Food: 

The benefits of halal food are beyond religious practice. Here are some of its benefits that make halal food a safer, balanced, and ethical food choice. 

Pure, Hygienic Meat: 

In halal meat, the blood is drained completely to ensure there are no bacteria or parasitic infestation, because blood is the main medium for their growth. Halal meat stays fresh for days, and it’s more safer option. 

Fresh and Safe:

Halal food is beyond meat; it’s a complete lifestyle that promotes fresh and safe health choices. The halal food focuses on: 

  • Lean meat ( chicken, beef) 
  • Fresh veggies. 
  • Whole grain (wheat, rice) 
  • Natural spices and herbs. 

Humane Treatment of Animals: 

Islam gives a stress-free and just slaughtering system. Halal slaughtering ensures: 

  • It grows and nature with halal food and water. 
  • It stays healthy and calm. 
  • The animal stays in good condition. 
  • It remains stress-free during slaughtering. 

The meat of a calm animal is softer, cleaner, and delicious.  Because when the animal gets stressed during slaughtering, it produces lactic acid in the body that reduces its taste. 

No Harmful Chemicals or Additives: 

Processed meat often has preservatives and artificial flavors. According to Islamic guidelines, it’s not allowed to add pork products, alcohol, and harmful artificial ingredients just for the taste. As a result, the meat remains healthy and safe. 

Nutritional Benefits of Halal Food: 

  1. Halal meat is rich in highquality protein, vital minerals like iron, zinc, selenium, and B vitamins. 
  2. Animals slaughtered in accordance with halal methods undergo less stress, which means fewer stress hormones and better meat quality. 

What’s Halal Meat? 

Halal meat is the one prepared according to Islamic dietary legislation. It includes slaughtering halal animals (cow, hen, goat, sheep, lamb, camel, etc) in a way that’s free from impurities, involves cutting the animal’s throat with a sharp knife that minimizes suffering, and the butcher must recite Bismillah while slaughtering. All these steps ensured that the meat is halal and more beneficial.

Benefits of Halal Meat: 

Halal meat has a positive impact on overall body health. 

  1. The slaughtering method of halal meat ensures that the animal is healthy and free from disease or pathogen infestation. 
  2. The blood of the animal is drained completely, reducing the risk of foodborne disease. 
  3. Halal meat is free from harmful additives, preservatives, and chemicals. 

According to a study conducted by Science Direct:

Red meat is important for proper body functioning. It clearly states that irrespective of the religion, Halal meat is becoming an interesting choice. 

The study further says that:

The healthier lipid profile in halal meat has a positive impact on the weight, muscle mass, body fat, and antioxidant status of the body. 

Taste of Halal Meat: 

Every living being on earth values its life; the same is true for animals. When they sense danger, their stress level increases, which in turn increases lactic acid that negatively impacts their taste. 

That’s when Islamic guidelines play a key role. For the meat to be halal, the blood is drained completely; as a result, all the negative stressors are completely drained, and this results in cleaner meat with no impurities that taste better than the meat slaughtered otherwise. 

The majority agrees that halal meat tastes better, but it’s something subjective and can vary based on a person’s taste profile and priorities. Anyhow, halal meat is believed to have a premium buttery texture and melt-in-the-mouth taste because of its preparation methods. 

Is Halal Food Good For Weight Loss? 

Halal food encourages healthy eating, especially if focused on lean meats, fresh veggies, whole grains, and minimal processed food. But for weight loss, it’s important you consider overall calorie intake, portion size, exercise, and required nutrition. Halal food itself doesn’t guarantee weight loss, but it’s about mindful eating with healthy choices that can make halal food helpful in weight loss. 

Difference Between Halal and Kosher:

Kosher is the Jewish dietary system and has some similarities with Islamic principles: 

  • Pork is impermissible in both. 
  • Both have specific slaughtering roles. 
  • Islam obligates us to take Allah’s name before slaughtering. 
  • Kosher has strict rules about mixing meat and dairy. 
  • The knife cuts are different.

FAQs: 

Is Halal Humane? 

No. Islam emphasizes kindness to animals and protects animal rights. The slaughtering system of Islam is based on kindness. Islam clearly guides that: When you slaughter, slaughter in a good way, your knife should be sharp enough so that the animal suffers the minimum. So, the Islamic slaughter system is humane and ethical. 

Is Halal Food Fattening? 

Not necessarily, it depends on the method of cooking. Being halal doesn’t mean fattening; it just means the food is processed according to Islamic principles. The fat content primarily depends on the food type and the cooking methods. 

Is Halal Food More Expensive? 

Halal foods are healthy and focus on quality, which is why they might seem a bit expensive because their production is on a smaller scale in the UK and the USA. Also, the certification procedure and separate processing system make their price slightly higher. 

Can Non Muslims Eat Halal Food? 

Yes, anyone who prioritizes healthy food can eat halal food irrespective of religion. 

Is Halal Chicken Healthy? 

Yes, halal chicken, if produced and processed according to true Islamic guidelines, it’s not only healthy but also a great source of lean, high-protein, and relatively low saturated fat (depending on the cooking methods).
