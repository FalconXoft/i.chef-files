Abir L.

We tried Rocky Point twice now. One was a relatively early mid-week dinner and the second time was a late dinner at 9:00pm on a Friday. The two experiences were kinda different from an ambience perspective but the food was great in both times. Mid week music was nice and the restaurant was quiet. Friday, it was super loud, I was yelling to order and I was not able to have a conversation with my husband. So if you don't like loud music, nights on a weekend are probably not for you. Restaurant is spacious and there's a big bar area as well. Service is superb! They are fast, they check on you regularly and are very pleasant. The menu is pretty extensive with whole fish options, fish fillet, shrimps, octopus, crab, etc. there are also fajita dishes, tacos, many appetizers to choose from and seafood cocktail dishes so there is something for everyone! We ordered multiple dishes so far but our favorites were the Ranchero fish fillet and the breaded shrimp. The dishes usually come with rice and potatoes and you get to choose either corn or flour tortillas. Flour is our favorite and theirs are very good. The potatoes are basically two slices of very good fried potatoes. The ranchero is so delicious. The fish fillet is huge and it is smothered in cooked onions, peppers and jalapeños so it's a tiny bit spicy but delicious. It is also served with salad. The same sides come with the breaded shrimp. It was cooked perfectly. We are fans of Jamaica. Theirs is refreshing without it being too sweet. They serve chips and salsa as soon as your arrive. Chips is fresh and salsa is medium heat to me. When you order, they get sauces and limes to the table. Prices are a little on the higher side but still acceptable. It is understandable because it's seafood. First time we paid $135. The second time the bill was $81 for 2 jamaicas, 2 rancheros, 1 breaded shrimp and a side of half an avocado. We will be returning because we are enjoying the whole experience every time!
