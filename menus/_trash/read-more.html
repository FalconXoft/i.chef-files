Italian sandwiches in Harrison are Jeanne Muchnick's Best Thing She Ate This Week

Jeanne Muchnick
Rockland/Westchester Journal News
Updated Oct. 4, 2024, 1:55 p.m. ET

I'm drawn to Italian food. And one of the places that does Italian food best is Casa D'italia Sandwich Shoppe in Harrison.

Their chicken vodka parm sandwich with melted mozzarella on toasted garlic bread was the best thing I ate this week.

It was just the right amount of ooey and gooey thanks to the melted cheese, as well as perfectly saucy (with more sauce on the side), and the chicken cooked just so. Plus wow to the bread which sopped up all its goodness.

I also loved the Michelangelo (Parma prosciutto, fresh mozzarella, broccoli rabe and roasted peppers) and the Mamma Maria (fried eggplant, fresh mozz and roasted peppers), the latter of which I tried because I went with my vegetarian friend.

Topped off with a cappuccino and cannoli (it's hard to resist the sweets on display here) and you're in Italian food heaven.

The husband-and-wife-owned spot, in business since 1997, is so popular and well known for its sandwiches, it can take about 20 minutes during the lunchtime rush to get your order in. The fact that so many people wait and often schmooze with Lucy and Steve Selvaggio (they seem to know everyone!) is not only a testament to the neighborhood vibe of their eatery, but to the fact that what they offer is really good.

Lucy the chef behind the hot food credits their popularity to their high quality ingredients, which either come direct from Arthur Avenue in the Bronx, where she and Steve both hail from, as well as to the Italian distributor they use. It helps, too, that both know (and appreciate) the flavors of true Italian food, thanks in part to their upbringing and to their parents, both of whom are Italian immigrants.

Among the sandwiches that showcase a nod to their roots: The Avenue (ham, sweet Soppressata, imported sharp provolone and broccoli rabe); Italian Flag (broccoli rabe, fresh mozzarella and roasted peppers); Italian Stallion (ham, salami, imported Mortadella, sweet Soppressata, provolone and roasted peppers); Bronx Bomber (roast beef, melted cheddar, sauteed peppers and onion on toasted garlic bread) and the Mona Lisa (smoked turkey, melted Brie, green apple and honey mustard). You can also create your own.

The two have poured their heart and soul into the 20-seat spot, which feels like a taste of the Old Country. In addition to their hot and cold sandwiches there are about 30 varieties served on either a club roll or hero (which some may call a wedge) they offer Italian cookies, cannoli (filled with ricotta cream), cappuccino, espresso and a host of imported Italian goods. (The shop originally began as a mini market before branching into hot and cold food.) You'll also find salads and steam-table specials.

Again, it bears repeating that this is no ordinary sandwich shop. The Harrison residents don't skimp on anything and have gone to great lengths to ensure everything tastes to their standards. That means putting a water filtration system in their basement specifically for creating the best possible processing for their coffee and cheese.

It also means Steve makes their milky, silky mozzarella daily (sometimes multiple times!) and the coffee is Esse. Bread comes from Terranova Bakery on Arthur Avenue.

Also their sandwiches are big: as in I took half home to have later for dinner. I also got more sauce to go it's seriously addictive.

If you go

Address: 265 Halstead Ave., Harrison, 914-835-6370; casaditalia.com.

Hours: 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Tuesday to Saturday.

Prices: Sandwiches are in the $10 range for a roll; $13 to $16 for a hero.

Good to know: To avoid the lunch crush, try ordering online. Or go at an off hour, like 10 a.m. Also good to know: they sell their sauces to go. (I highly recommend the vodka sauce.)
